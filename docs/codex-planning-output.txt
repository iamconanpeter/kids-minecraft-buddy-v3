As of **February 20, 2026**, here’s a practical design brief.

1. **Comparable landscape (Android, iOS, browser/online)**
- **Android UGC/social-heavy:** `Roblox` (Teen, 1B+ downloads, IAP), `PK XD` (Everyone 10+, 100M+ downloads, ads + IAP), `Minecraft` (Everyone 10+, 50M+, paid + IAP).  
- **iOS split by safety posture:** `Roblox` (13+, UGC/chat/loot-box descriptors), `Minecraft` (9+, paid + IAP), `Toca Boca World` (4+, single-player, ad-free claim, heavy IAP).
- **Browser/online worlds:** `Minecraft Classic` runs in-browser (keyboard/WebGL/WebRTC required), `KoGaMa` positions itself as browser-first UGC with multiplayer + creation.
- **Inference from this landscape:** For ages 6-7, the biggest whitespace is **Minecraft-like creativity + Toca-like safety defaults**, before opening full UGC/social risk.

2. **Why kids love these loops (psychology)**
- **Autonomy:** Kids can choose “what to build next,” which increases intrinsic motivation.
- **Competence:** Quick mastery loops (build, solve, upgrade) produce strong “I can do this” momentum.
- **Relatedness:** Buddy play and co-op goals satisfy social connection, even with light AI companions.
- **Pretend-play identity:** Roleplay (builder, explorer, hero) supports creativity, language, and social skill practice.
- **Creator-style attachment:** Kids form para-social bonds with funny/high-energy personalities; character-driven “buddy narration” boosts return behavior.

3. **Five moat opportunities (legally safer block-play buddy app)**
- **Trust moat:** Ship with no open chat, no external DMs, parent dashboard, spending caps, and session controls from day one.
- **Co-play moat:** “2-buddy adventures” that require turn-taking puzzles (parent/child or sibling), not just solo grind.
- **Content moat:** Procedural mission remix engine (same blocks, infinite kid-safe stories) so novelty doesn’t rely on risky UGC.
- **Safety-tech moat:** Template-based sharing only (no free text/images by default), with pre-moderated asset libraries.
- **Ethics moat:** Transparent rewards (no loot-box pressure), clear earn paths, and parent-visible progression logs.

4. **Legal-safe translation of JJ & Mikey-style patterns**
- **Pattern: high-energy duo chaos** -> **Translate:** original buddy duo archetypes with your own names, art bible, voice style.
- **Pattern: escalating “rescue/challenge” episodes** -> **Translate:** short mission arcs (5-8 min) with clear start-win-reset loop.
- **Pattern: prank/surprise moments** -> **Translate:** slapstick world events with no humiliation, no unsafe imitation prompts.
- **Pattern: creator callouts/catchphrases** -> **Translate:** brand-new catch-lines and UI language; never reuse signature phrases.
- **Pattern: “watch me do this” social pull** -> **Translate:** in-app replay ghosts and parent-approved challenge cards, not open social feeds.

**Non-negotiable guardrails**
- Don’t copy names, thumbnails, skins, logos, or channel-identifying motifs (copyright/trademark risk).
- Assume kids under 13 handling must satisfy COPPA updates (including stricter ad-data consent rules).
- Align with Apple Kids category and Google Play Families requirements before feature scope freeze.
- Treat this as product guidance, then get counsel review before launch.
