# PRD — Kids Minecraft Buddy v3

## 1. Product summary
Kids Minecraft Buddy v3 is a **native Android** game for ages 6–7+.
It combines block placement, buddy-led mini-adventures, and gentle progression in short sessions.

Core promise:
- Kids: “I can build cool stuff with my buddy and go on adventures.”
- Parents: “It is safe, easy to understand, and not designed to trap my child.”

## 2. Goals
1. Deliver unmistakable Minecraft-inspired block-play feel with original IP.
2. Deliver JJ & Mikey-style duo-adventure pacing in legal-safe form.
3. Keep onboarding readable by low-literacy players.
4. Provide fair progression and replayability without addictive pressure.

## 3. Non-goals
- No online multiplayer.
- No open user-generated content sharing.
- No external chat.
- No web implementation.

## 4. Audience
- Primary: kids 6–9, especially 6–7 early readers.
- Secondary: parents who value safe, short-form play.

## 5. Core game loop
1. Enter home hub.
2. Buddy suggests mission via icon card.
3. Player enters mission map.
4. Collect block energy + place/remove blocks to solve mission objective.
5. Complete mission, receive stars and unlock token.
6. Return to hub, spend rewards on buddy stickers/colors.
7. Repeat with increased challenge variety.

Session target: 5–10 minutes.

## 6. Key features

### 6.1 Buddy system (required)
- At least one buddy character (v1 in app: **Pip**).
- Buddy behaviors:
  - mission suggestion,
  - contextual encouragement,
  - completion celebration.

### 6.2 Mission/adventure loop (required)
- Mission types:
  - Build Bridge,
  - Light Path,
  - Shelter Build,
  - Buddy Rescue.
- Each mission has clear visual objective icon + progress meter.

### 6.3 Progression + local saves (required)
- Stars for mission completion.
- Level progress based on stars earned.
- Local save tracks unlocked missions, stars, buddy cosmetic unlocks.

### 6.4 Child-friendly UX
- Icon-first navigation.
- Very short text labels.
- Large buttons/touch targets.
- High contrast, clear color coding.

## 7. Onboarding for low literacy
- Step 1: Tap glowing mission card.
- Step 2: Tap green grid to place block.
- Step 3: Long press to remove block.
- Step 4: Reach objective icon to finish.

Rules:
- Use gestures + animation hints first.
- Keep text optional and < 6 words where possible.

## 8. Control scheme for small hands
- Primary interaction: single tap for place.
- Secondary interaction: long press for remove.
- Horizontal palette with large block-type icons.
- No tiny joystick requirement in v1.

## 9. Fairness principles
- No failure punishment that removes earned stars.
- Mission retries are free.
- Difficulty increases by objective count, not hidden stat inflation.
- Rewards are deterministic and clearly shown.

## 10. Retention without addiction guardrails
- No loot boxes.
- No streak punishment.
- No infinite random reward cycles.
- Session-end celebration after each mission encourages natural stopping points.
- “Take a break” nudge after several missions.

## 11. Success criteria (v1)
- User can finish first mission within 2 minutes.
- At least 3 mission completions available in first play session.
- Save/load preserves progress after app restart.
- Core loop is understandable without reading full sentences.

## 12. Explicit answers required by stakeholder

### Q1: Where is Minecraft feel in this game?
- Grid-based block placement/removal.
- Build structures to solve objectives.
- Block resource economy and biome-style mission maps.
- Sandbox flavor inside guided mission loop.

### Q2: How is this inspired by JJ & Mikey-style content without IP infringement?
- We use the duo-adventure pacing and emotional structure only.
- Characters, names, dialogue, iconography, and mission narratives are fully original.
- No direct visual or textual references to known creator IP.
